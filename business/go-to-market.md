# Go-to-Market Strategy

## Overview

ELOS uses a community-led, content-first growth strategy. The target user (competitive high school student) lives on TikTok, Reddit, and Discord. They trust peers and authenticity, not ads. Our GTM matches that.

**North star**: 10,000 paid users within 18 months of App Store launch.

---

## Phase 0 — Pre-Launch (Now → App Store approval)

### Goal: 500 waitlist signups, 100 TestFlight beta users

**Tactics:**
1. **Personal network seeding**: Share with every person in Frank's school, gym, and sports team. Target: 50 real beta users who use ELOS daily.

2. **Reddit posts (organic)**:
   - r/fitness: "I'm a high school student and I built an app to replace MyFitnessPal + Notion + my school planner — here's what I learned"
   - r/learnprogramming: "Built a full SwiftUI fitness app in high school"
   - r/StudentAthletes: "How I track workouts + grades in one app (built it myself)"
   - Format: Value-first post, ELOS mentioned naturally, link to waitlist only if asked
   
3. **Twitter/X thread**: "I'm a high school student who logs workouts, nutrition, sleep, and grades in one app I built myself. Here's what I learned building a real iOS app as a teenager [thread]"

4. **TestFlight beta invite post**: Create a TestFlight link, post in relevant Discord servers (study Discord, fitness Discord, PPL subreddit)

5. **Waitlist landing page** (elos.app or elos.vercel.app):
   - Email capture
   - "Notify me when it launches"
   - Simple, shows 3 screenshots
   - No login required

## Phase 1 — Launch (Months 1–3)

### Goal: 1,000 downloads, 100 paid users, 4.7+ App Store rating

**App Store Launch Day:**
- Post on all channels simultaneously (Reddit, Twitter, TikTok, Instagram, Product Hunt)
- Get 10 friends/beta users to leave 5-star reviews on launch day (critical for App Store algorithm)
- Submit to Product Hunt: "ELOS — The first app built for serious student athletes"

**Product Hunt tactics:**
- Launch on a Tuesday/Wednesday (peak PH days)
- Have 30 supporters lined up to upvote at 12:01 AM Pacific
- Goal: #1 Product of the Day → huge email newsletter exposure

**Content creation (TikTok/Instagram Reels):**

Format 1: "Day in my life" (highest performing fitness content)
- 60-second video: Wake up → show ELOS Today dashboard → gym → log workout in ELOS → class → log lunch with AI → study → log sleep
- No hard sell. Just show the product working

Format 2: "I built this"
- "POV: You're a 17-year-old who built his own iPhone app because MyFitnessPal was annoying"
- Behind-the-scenes of building → show final product
- Authenticity is the differentiator

Format 3: Before/After
- "Before ELOS: 6 apps open at once [screenshot]. After ELOS: [ELOS Today screen]"

**Posting cadence**: 3× per week minimum for first 90 days. Consistency > virality.

**Discord seeding**:
- Find every fitness/study Discord with >1,000 members
- Post in #apps or #recommendations channels naturally
- Don't spam — add value to conversations first, then mention ELOS when relevant

## Phase 2 — Community-Led Growth (Months 4–9)

### Goal: 5,000 downloads, 500 paid, $3K MRR

**Student ambassador program:**

Recruit 20 "ELOS Ambassadors" — one at each major school/campus:
- Requirements: Uses ELOS daily, posts 1 piece of content/week about it
- Benefits: Free Pro forever, early access, $5 per paid referral (tracked via referral link)
- How to find them: DM the most engaged beta users, post in Discord

**School athletic department outreach:**
- Identify 10 high schools with strong athletic programs
- Email athletic director / head strength coach:
  > "I'm a [grade] student who built a free app for athletes. I'd love to give your team a free 30-day trial. Would 10 minutes to see it work?"
- Objective: Team license deal ($4.99/athlete/month × 20 athletes = $99/month per school)
- Secondary objective: Get school to mention ELOS in their newsletter / Instagram → free distribution

**Press strategy:**
- Target reporters who cover "teen entrepreneurs" and "edtech":
  - Business Insider: "Gen Z Founders" vertical
  - TechCrunch: "Startup" submissions
  - Your local newspaper / school newspaper (always says yes, gets linked from regional press)
  - The key hook: High school student builds app that replaces 5 apps for athletes — and it's actually good
- PR pitch template in [investor-faq.md](investor-faq.md)

**App Store optimization (ASO):**
- Keywords to rank for: "student fitness", "workout tracker", "student planner", "college fitness", "athlete tracker", "AI meal tracker"
- A/B test screenshots: Workout logging vs. Today dashboard as first screenshot
- Update screenshots and description monthly based on download → conversion data

**Referral program launch:**
- "Give a friend 1 month Pro, get 1 month Pro"
- Shareable link visible on Me tab
- Track with a referral tracking service (RevenueCat has built-in referral tracking)

## Phase 3 — Paid Acquisition + Scale (Months 10–18)

### Goal: 10,000 paid, $60K MRR

**Paid social (only after organic is working):**
- Don't start paid until CAC (organic) < $8 and D30 retention > 30%
- Starting budget: $2K/month
- Channels: TikTok Ads, Instagram Ads
- Creative: UGC-style (looks like organic content, not an ad)
- Targeting: 16–24, fitness interest, student interest, iOS users only
- Attribution: Use RevenueCat + Adjust for mobile attribution

**App Store Search Ads:**
- Start with exact match on competitor keywords: "myfitnesspal alternative", "workout tracker student"
- Budget: $500/month to start
- CPI target: <$3

**Influencer partnerships (micro-influencers, not mega):**
- Target: Student athletes with 5K–50K followers on TikTok/Instagram
- Rate: $200–500 per video (or equity equivalent for long-term)
- Requirement: Must use ELOS for 1 week before filming content
- Track with unique discount code per creator (30% off first month)
- Avoid mega-influencers — too expensive, wrong demographic alignment

| Track & field / swim team | Coach outreach | "Team leaderboards + progress reports for free" |

## Launch Checklist

**Two weeks before launch:**
- [ ] App Store listing: screenshots, description, keywords finalized
- [ ] Press kit ready: logo, screenshots, founder bio, one-liner, stats
- [ ] 30 App Store review supporters confirmed (friends, beta users)
- [ ] Product Hunt hunters confirmed
- [ ] TikTok/Instagram content scheduled (launch week: 5 posts)
- [ ] Beta users emailed: "App launches in 2 weeks — please review it on day 1"

**Launch day:**
- [ ] Submit to App Store (keep "Publish immediately" selected)
- [ ] Post on TikTok, Instagram, Reddit, Twitter at 9 AM EST
- [ ] Launch Product Hunt at 12:01 AM Pacific
- [ ] DM everyone who was on the waitlist with the App Store link
- [ ] Post in every Discord server you're in

**Week 1 post-launch:**
- [ ] Respond to every App Store review
- [ ] Fix any P0 bugs within 24 hours and push update
- [ ] Track D1 retention (if <40%, something is wrong with onboarding)
- [ ] Post "Day 1 launch recap" on social (be authentic — numbers + learnings)
